Healthy Workplaces. Healthy Workers. Healthy Communities.

The MFL Occupational Health Centre (OHC) is a community health centre funded by Winnipeg Regional Health Authority and donations. The Centre helps workers, employers, and joint health and safety committees to improve workplace health and safety conditions and eliminate hazards.

QNET Work Matters Conference May 17th

Date:
May 17, 2016 | 7:30 am - 4:30 pm

Location:
Victoria Inn | 1808 Wellington Ave.

Explore what makes work meaningful to you and your team at the 2016 QNET Work Matters Conference on May 17 in Winnipeg. Featuring renowned international speaker and best-selling author of Do More Great Work, Michael Bungay Stanier, this year’s conference puts the spotlight on finding and creating the work that can inspire, empower and engage.  Breakout sessions in the area of Leadership, Quality, Wellness and Tools and Techniques allow you to create a conference day that is meaningful for you.

Michael Bungay Stanier in Winnipeg  

Michael Bungay Stanier, a renowned international speaker and best-selling author of Do More Great Work, will engage and inspire you with a provocative keynote to open the 2016 QNET Work Matters Conference on May 17 at the Victoria Inn, Winnipeg. Michael will introduce the theme for the Work Matters conference by discussing the work that has impact, the work that has meaning, the work that makes a difference.

Featuring more than 20 speakers in breakout sessions focused on Leadership, Quality, Wellness and Tools & Techniques, the Work Matters conference allows you to create a conference day that is meaningful for you.

For those of you who “tweet” please follow @QNETManitoba. We will be using the hashtag #QNET2016 for everything related to this year’s conference – please join the conversation!

May 24th Free Public Presentation - Injury Prevention: A New Twist On An Old Classic

Date:
May 24, 2016 | 9:00 am - 10:00 am

Location:
Room 103 – Union Centre | 275 Broadway

Occupational Health Centre is hosting a free public presentation in May:

Injury Prevention – A New Twist On An Old Classic

Presented by Sylvia!

Occupational Therapist

MindBody Works

poster

 It is very important to have a good safety and health program for workplaces.  Sylvia will share about a program she has developed for manufacturing that provides onsite injury management, injury prevention, physical/occupational assessment and treatment, ergonomic intervention and workplace modification.

The goal of the program is to help individuals with symptoms before they become syndromes, keep them happy and healthy at work, improve their quality of work and home life, prevent injury and reduce time loss.  Sylvia also provides services to other industries:  healthcare, office, government and labour.

Sylvia is an entertaining speaker who gives her audience the tools to build healthy lives and workplaces.  Her presentation focus on a proactive approach to building healthy lives and work places. Sylvia is an Occupational Therapist with years of assessing and treating injuries, she offers a unique insight through which she has developed a proactive approach to preventing stress and workplace injuries.  She is also specializing in workplace injury prevention.

For more information, please contact Tiffany at 204-926-7909, or tiffany.pau@mflohc.mb.ca

June 14th Free Public Presentation - The Mysteries of Indoor Air Quality

Date:
Jun 14, 2016 | 9:15 am - 10:15 am

Location:
Room 2B – Union Centre | 275 Broadway

OHC offers a free public presentation in May:

The Mysteries of Indoor Air Quality

Presented by:

Alison Reineke, Occupational Hygienist

Elias Occupational Hygiene Consulting

When you and staff have questions and concerns regarding indoor air quality  (IAQ) in your building, were do you start?   There are a number of possible sources and locations to inspect, including the ventilation system, water leaks, cleaning/maintenance chemicals and allergens.

Safety committees, maintenance workers, and fellow workers can all play a part to help solve the mystery.
