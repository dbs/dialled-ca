Facilities

The Faculty of Information & Media Studies is home to facilities designed to enhance the learning of students, through access to materials, equipment and active spaces.

New building coming in January 2017



Construction is well underway on a brand new facility that will house both FIMS and Nursing (Faculty of Health Sciences). After several years bursting at the seams in North Campus Building, the FIMS community will move en masse to the 130,000 sq. ft. facility which was designed with the input of FIMS faculty and staff. The space will include media studios, flex space, an expanded Graduate Resource Centre, and common areas for gathering and interacting, with a premium on seating and break-out areas for students. Natural light is also an important feature, with large walls of windows found on all sides of the building.

Classes are scheduled to begin in the new building in January 2017.

Graduate Resource Centre

The Graduate Resource Centre (GRC) is a special library currently located in room 280, North Campus Building, Western University. We support all students, faculty, and staff involved in graduate education in the Faculty of Information and Media Studies at Western. We provide access to electronic databases and print collections, including books, periodicals, audiovisual resources and equipment, and course reserves. Our staff deliver instructional workshops through "The GRC Presents…", answer reference questions, and offer computing support for the labs.

Location: On-site at FIMS, North Campus Building, Room 280
Hours: Mon-Thurs: 8:30am - 7:00pm, Friday: 8:30am - 5:00pm, Sat/Sun: 1:00pm - 5:00pm
Subjects: Health Information Science, Journalism, Media Studies, Popular Music & Culture, Library & Information Science

Access: While the GRC primarily supports FIMS graduate students and faculty, we welcome the Western community, alumni, and external researchers and practitioners. Resources do not circulate and are for in-house use only. GRC holdings are included in the Western Libraries catalogue.
