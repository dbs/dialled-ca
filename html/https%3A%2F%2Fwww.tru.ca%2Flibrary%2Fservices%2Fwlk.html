  • TRU Library has over 250,000 volumes of books, 12,000 films and videos, 105 online article databases, 120,000+ ebooks, and subscriptions to more than 34,000 electronic and print periodicals. All materials are available to TRU Library: Williams Lake Branch.

    The library is located in the Library Centre accessed through the TRU Williams Lake Bookstore.

     

    Hours:

    Library hours change during the weeks following exams and for the summer months.  Please check the library's website for current library hours or with the Library Centre Student Services Clerk at 250-392-8030 during those dates. 

  • Computers

    There are eight (8) networked computers in the library.  Users are required to logon using their TRU credentials when using these computers

    Logon Information

    To logon to these computers you must have your myTRU email address.  The first part of your myTRU email address serves as your User Name.

    What is myTRU?

    myTRU allows students access to TRU's student record system.  All students will be able to pay fees, print their schedule, look up grades, access their TRU Email, Moodle and Blackboard account.   Your myTRU account has a link for your personal TRU email.  Check regularly for important notices.  You can forward your TRU emails to one of your other emails accounts if you like (example:  hotmail).  Note:  New students - your myTRU account will be active once you have been admitted to TRU. To find your myTRU email address, see this page for details.

    Logon Format to use for all computers

    Student

    Username: use first part of your myTRU email address which is commonly your first initial & last name; may also include a number (e.g. jsmith2) Password default: Your birthdate in the format dd-Mmm-yy (e.g. 20-Aug-87)*
    *You must change your password when you log on for the first time. Passwords must have at least one capital letter, at least one small letter, at least one number, and be 8 or more characters in length.

    Faculty/Staff*

    Username: first initial & last name; may also include a number (e.g. jsmith2) Password: assigned by Information Technology Services**
    *Departments usually apply for a network account for all Faculty and staff. **You must change your password when you logon for the first time. Passwords must have at least one capital letter, at least one small letter, at least one number, and be 8 or more characters in length.

    Blackboard and Moodle

    Blackboard and Moodle are the learning management systems used at TRU.  Learn more about these systems at Information Technology Service.

    Print, Scan, Copy

    All computers in the library print to a designated in-library printer.  Users must supply their own printer paper.  A Multi-Function Printer is available for users who wish to scan items to email or save on a USB key, or print ($0.10 per page).

  • TRU Library: Williams Lake Branch provides access to the same materials as are available at other TRU Library branches. Many of those resources are available online.

    Search TRU Library Resources Using Our Discovery Service

    Our discovery search, the search box found on the library's homepage, is the Library's most comprehensive search engine. Fast and powerful, it is a good starting point no matter what your topic. Use it to find information in any discipline and pretty much any format: journal articles, print and electronic books, dissertations, maps, CDs and DVDs and much more.

    Journals and Articles

    TRU Library subscribes to the dozens of licensed resources. You can find a complete list of those databases from the Article Database tab on the TRU Library’s home page.

    Library Catalogue

    The TRU Library catalogue offers many search tools and library services.  You can request items through the Interlibrary Loan Service, place holds, renew your checked-out items, check your library account, and check course reserves.

    Videos & DVDs

    TRU Library has access to thousands of streaming videos for use by students, faculty, and staff. Collections are approved for classroom use unless otherwise specified. Many videos feature closed captioning (CC). If available, a  button will appear in the video player. Click this button to toggle captions on/off. See the National Film Board of Canada or Theatre in Video. To access electronic resources off-campus, you must authenticate yourself by entering your Student ID number and your Library PIN. Your Library PIN is usually your birthdate in this format: yymmdd

    Images

    ARTstor Digital Library provides over 1.6 million digital images in the arts, architecture, humanities, and sciences with an accessible suite of software tools for teaching and research. Our community-built collections comprise contributions from outstanding international museums, photographers, libraries, scholars, photo archives, and artists and artists' estates.

  • Services

    While in the library you can:

    • checkout items from reserves or stacks
    • receive items you have requested through the Interlibrary Loan Service
    • participate in on-going workshops (for example: Creating PowerPoint Presentations, Conquering Google Searches, Leverage Google Scholar, Finding Scholarly Articles)
    • borrow a video camera
    • watch a VHS at the Viewing Station
    • consult with a tutor at the Writing Centre
    • consult with the branch librarian for all your research and information needs

    Space

    The TRU Library: Williams Lake Branch provides a variety of areas where students can study and learn. The library has:

    • two (2) group study rooms that can be conveniently booked online. Click here.
    • eight (8) study carrels for private quiet study space
    • five (5) group study tables for active learning
    • a lounge area with sofa and chairs
    • one (1) VHS /DVD viewing station
    • eight (8) networked computers, two (2) express computers (not requiring logon credentials)
    • one (1) Reference & Help Desk where students connect with the branch librarian for studying and research support

    The library has the following collections in the physical space:
    • Oversized items
    • Classic and vintage books
    • TRU Reads novels (classics and new)
    • Stacks
    • Newspapers
    • Periodicals
    • Audio Visuals

  • During the summer, library research support and help can be obtained by phoning : 1-800-663-1699 or emailing reference@tru.ca.
